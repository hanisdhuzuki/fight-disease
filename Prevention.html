PREVENTION

Healthy habits can prevent germs and infectious diseases from spreading. People can reduce their risk of contracting or transmitting diseases causing phantogen by following the step below:

Wash Hands Properly

The usage of soap when washing hands is important in order to kill the bacteria. This is especially important before and after preparing foods, before eating and after using the toilet.

Practice Good Hygiene When
Handling Food

Keep counters and other kitchen surfaces clean when preparing meals. In addition, promptly refrigerate leftovers. Don't let cooked foods remain at room temperature for an extended period of time.

Clean and Disinfect Commonly
Used Surface

The commonly used surfaces such as include the kitchen and bathroom should be cleaned and difinfect frequently as they are the two rooms that can have a high concentration of bacteria and other infectious agents.

Get Vaccinated

Immunization can drastically reduce the chances of contracting many diseases. Keep your recommended vaccinations up-to-date. Vaccinating not only protects yourself, but also protects those in the community who are unable to be vaccinated.

Don't Share Personal Belongings

Avoid sharing personal items that can’t be disinfected, like toothbrushes and razors, or sharing towels between washes. This is to avoid the transmission of fungal diseases.

Stay Home When Sick

Don't go to work or class if you're vomiting, have diarrhea or are running a fever. Get some rest to make you get well sooner and you will not spread any germs.

TREATMENT

Some communicable diseases cause only mild symptoms that disappear without treatment. Others may cause severe symptoms, or potentially life threatening complications. The treatment for such diseases depends on whether they are bacterial, viral, or fungal.
